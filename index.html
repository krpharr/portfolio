About Me

Born on Titan, the second moon of Saturn, and unwittingly brought to earth by an accident with a wormhole, I was adopted and raised by a pack of wolves in the mountains of southwest Virginia. After a chance encounter with interstellar jazz musicians in my early teens, I gleened my means of escape and prospects of living my life as a musician.

From 1988 to 1992, I attended the Jazz Studies program at Virginia Commonwealth Univerisity and did well in music but failed miserably at college because wolves know nothing about going to college. Because of my professional experience I was hired by Virginia Commonwealth Univerisity in 2005 to teach upright and electric jazz bass, jazz improvisation, and small jazz ensembles. In 2011, I was also hired to teach jazz bass at the University of Richmond.

Another passion of mine is learning computer languages. I studied Basic in my early teens on a Commodore Vic 20 and picked back up in my twenties with C and C++ and then again in my early forties with Python. After 30 years of working self-employed as a professional musician and 14 years part-time adjunct faculty, I am looking for more sustainable employment for my age by strengthening my skills as a programmer and gaining the education and experience needed to be gainfully employed.
